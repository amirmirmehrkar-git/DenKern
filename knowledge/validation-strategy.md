## Tier 1 Questions

- What financial outcome would make the pilot obviously worth paying for?

## Core Interview Prompt

Do not start with "Would you use AI?"

Start with:

> Tell me about the last time a shipment delay threatened production.

Then ask:

- What happened operationally?
- Who got involved?
- How long did decisions take?
- What did the delay cost?
- What options did you consider?

If this conversation becomes emotionally specific, the pain is likely real.

## Interview Question Structure

Avoid generic conversations without a consistent structure. For each disruption story, capture:

- Situation: what was the last disruption?
- Decision: what decision was made?
- Data: what data was available at decision time?
- Pain: where was uncertainty highest?
- Risk: what felt risky (cost, delay, legal, penalties)?
- Aftermath: what had to be justified after the fact?

These cases become the core asset for product shaping, demo design, and later sales narratives.

## Missing Current Workflow Evidence

The biggest missing discovery item is the customer's current disruption workflow. The team needs to learn what Lena or an equivalent operator does today when disruption risk appears:

- Who escalates the issue?
- Who approves action?
- Which spreadsheets are used?
- Which ERP or SAP screenshots matter?
- How does procurement decide?
- Can the operations manager reorder directly?
- When does finance enter the decision?
- How fast does the decision need to happen?
- What is the current bottleneck?

DenkKern is competing with phone calls, Excel, SAP screenshots, email chains, intuition, and escalation meetings.

## Validation Target

The first validation work should prove whether the Lena 2.0 workflow is:

- Painful enough.
- Quantifiable enough.
- Actionable enough.
- Fast enough to onboard.
- Valuable enough to pay for.

## MVP KPI

The most important MVP KPI is:

```text
Did the customer make a faster and financially better disruption decision?
```

Dashboard usage, AI accuracy, and generic engagement metrics are secondary unless they support this outcome.
