# Internal Summary - Mentor Discussion With Nahid

Here is a summary of the key points and guidance received from Nahid regarding the current stage, interview approach, networking strategy, and use of available support channels.

## 1) Current Stage: Focus On Segment, Partners, And Painful Decision Gap

The biggest open questions are still defining:

- The right customer segment
- The right partner ecosystem
- The specific pain point / decision gap in the logistics value chain that is painful enough to solve

This requires more interviews. Some initial expert conversations and event attendance have already happened, but validation is still early.

## 2) Interview Approach: Individual Interviews Beat Team Interviews

Recommendation: conduct interviews individually (1:1), not as a group.

Reasons:

- People tend to give more honest and deeper answers in 1:1 conversations.
- Broader coverage: two people can do two separate interviews instead of joining one.
- Alignment and bias reduction should happen afterwards internally, not by attending interviews together.

To reduce bias and ensure internal alignment:

- Ask permission to record interviews when possible.
- If recording is not allowed, take detailed notes.
- Review recordings and notes internally as a team.

## 3) Networking / Community Subscription: Only Worth It If It Gives Direct Access

A logistics hub/community subscription is worth considering only if it provides direct access to the right people and can lead to multiple useful conversations.

Decision criteria:

- Does it give access to the exact roles we want to interview?
- Can it generate repeated conversations, not just exposure?

Funding note: to approve any subscription spend, Nahid would need:

- Subscription details
- Price
- Internal finance confirmation (budgets are being reviewed strictly)

## 4) Need For Stronger Alignment

Internal:

- Run a short weekly sync.
- Align on what each person learned from conversations.
- Align on where to focus next.

External:

- Avoid speaking to too many scattered profiles at once.
- Focus interviews on similar roles / similar user types first.
- Converge learnings, then expand to other segments if needed.

Key point: the learning process should converge, not get too broad too early.

## 5) Helping Other Startups / Using The Accelerator Network

Recommended channels:

- Use the cohort group channel.
- Use Slack DMs for direct peer outreach.
- For alumni and beyond the cohort, use broader community spaces (introductions, peer support, similar channels).

Offering help and asking for help is a good way to stay visible and connected.

## 6) How To Approach The Mentor Contact List

The snowball referral approach is fine: talk to one person, learn, ask for the next intro.

But the key requirement: be very clear internally on what we want from each person before reaching out.

Guidance:

- Do not go into meetings with a vague mindset like "we'll take whatever they can offer."
- Start by giving the other person the floor and learning what they care about.
- Adapt the framing during the conversation if needed, but still go in knowing our objective.

## 7) Referrals Should Be Intentional, Not Generic

Asking for referrals at the end of meetings is good, but be specific.

If possible:

- Mention names.
- Reference the person directly: "I saw you are connected to X" or "if you know Y, could you connect us?"

Takeaway: targeted referrals perform better than broad requests.

## Main Team Takeaways (Action Points)

- Do interviews individually, not as a team.
- Create a lightweight internal process to review interview notes/recordings together.
- Run a short weekly sync focused on market learnings and next interview targets.
- Narrow interview targets by role/profile first, instead of talking to everyone at once.
- Clarify what we want from each contact before every meeting.
- Use a snowball strategy with specific asks and specific referral targets.
- Evaluate any paid logistics community membership only if it gives direct access to relevant interviewees.
