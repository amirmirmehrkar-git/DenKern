# ICP And GTM

Source: Amir provided a GTM / ICP synthesis from another chat. Treat this as active ICP direction, not yet validated customer evidence.

## Versioned Wedge Notes

- Version 1 (manufacturing-first): Primary MVP wedge is manufacturing decision support (Lena 2.0) with downtime pain.
- Version 2 (shipping-first): For MVP, shipping / maritime disruption is the best wedge because external signals are more observable, data is easier to access, decisions are clearer, and map demos are more convincing.

Resolution status: unresolved. Keep both until the team explicitly decides which wedge leads the MVP narrative and build sequence.

## Versioned Trust Notes

- Version 1 (model-first intuition): "Build a strong model to earn trust, then customers share data."
- Version 2 (B2B reality framing): "Earn trust by demonstrating problem understanding and workflow fit, then get limited data access, then improve the product and model."

Resolution status: unresolved. Keep both until validated via customer discovery.

## Brutally Focused MVP Customer

The current customer is not:

- Every logistics company.
- The whole supply chain market.
- Enterprise operations broadly.
- Manufacturing broadly.

## Primary ICP

Operations-heavy manufacturers with high downtime cost and shipment dependency.

Best current beachhead:

> German industrial manufacturers with expensive production downtime caused by delayed imported components.

Narrower version:

> Hamburg-area manufacturers dependent on maritime supply chains and high-cost production continuity.

## User Versus Buyer

The company is not the only "customer" unit. DenkKern needs to distinguish user, buyer, influencer, and gatekeeper.

| Role | Type | Why important |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Production Manager | User | Pain owner |
| Supply Chain Manager | User / Influencer | Shipment coordination |
| Plant Manager | Economic buyer | Downtime accountability |
| COO / Operations Director | Budget owner | Operational KPI owner |
| Procurement Lead | Influencer | Reorder decisions |
| CIO / IT | Gatekeeper | Security and integration |

## Best Current Wedge

Plant operations / production leadership in companies where:

- Production stoppage is expensive.
- Parts are specialized.
- Delays have cascading impact.
- Replacement sourcing is hard.
- Shipping uncertainty matters.

## Strong ICP Signals

Look for companies with:

- EUR 50k-500k+ daily downtime risk.
- Imported components.
- Maritime shipping dependency.
- Specialized or non-substitutable parts.
- Low buffer inventory.
- Manual escalation workflow.
- SAP-heavy but decision-light workflows.
- Continued use of Excel, meetings, phone calls, and email chains for disruption decisions.

## Bad ICP For MVP

Avoid initially:

- Generic logistics providers.
- Pure logistics companies.
- Small manufacturers with low downtime costs.
- Low-margin commodity manufacturing.
- Companies with massive inventory buffers.
- ERP-integration-heavy enterprises.
- Companies needing deep ERP integration from day one.
- SMEs with low operational complexity and low willingness to pay.

Rationale: DenkKern optimizes operational impact and mitigation decisions, not just shipment movement or visibility.

## Real Customer Problem

Not:

> Where is my shipment?

But:

> What operational decision minimizes business loss right now?

## GTM Insight

DenkKern is selling:

> Faster operational disruption decisions under uncertainty.

Possible framing:

> Operational insurance intelligence.

Meaning: when disruption happens, the product helps minimize expensive mistakes.

This is stronger than "AI supply chain analytics" because it is specific, painful, financially measurable, and tied to action.

## Customer Interview Question

Do not lead with:

> Would you use AI?

Lead with:

> Tell me about the last time a shipment delay threatened production.

Follow-ups:

- What happened operationally?
- Who got involved?
- How long did decisions take?
- What did the delay cost?
- What options did you consider?

If this conversation becomes emotional and specific, that is a strong pain signal.

## Hapag-Lloyd Positioning

Hapag-Lloyd can be valuable, but not as the primary MVP customer.

Best current role:

- Strategic partner.
- Data partner.
- Distribution partner.
- Ecosystem validator.
- Future enterprise customer.

Why it is attractive:

- Understands maritime disruption.
- Has shipping data.
- Has ETA and visibility context.
- Sees customers affected by delays.
- Has enterprise scale.
- Is Hamburg-based.
- Has strong narrative alignment.

Why it is not the primary MVP customer:

- DenkKern is not currently a shipping optimization tool.
- The core value is production-loss minimization and operational mitigation decisions.
- Hapag-Lloyd sells shipping capacity, logistics visibility, container movement, ETA information, and maritime operations.
- DenkKern sells manufacturer-side decision support.

Best framing for a Hapag-Lloyd conversation:

> We help your industrial customers make better operational decisions during disruptions.

Potential future fit:

- Premium disruption intelligence service.
- Customer-facing decision layer.
- Enterprise visibility enhancement.
- Smart escalation tooling.
- Hapag ETA plus DenkKern decision layer.

Risk:

Going too early toward Hapag-Lloyd may create logistics platform gravity: visibility features, feature creep, integration-heavy work, and a drift away from the manufacturing decision-support MVP.

Priority order:

1. Manufacturers with downtime pain.
2. Industrial operators.
3. Logistics ecosystem partners.

Final status:

Hapag-Lloyd is an excellent strategic conversation and possible future channel, but not the first pilot and not the product-direction owner.
