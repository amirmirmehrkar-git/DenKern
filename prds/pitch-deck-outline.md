# Pitch Deck Structure (10 Slides)

Source: Amir provided a concise, professional outline for a 10-slide deck.

## Slide 1 - Title

Title: Decision Intelligence for Disruptions

Subtitle: Helping organizations make defensible decisions under uncertainty

Footer:

- Team name.
- Hamburg / Germany.

## Slide 2 - Problem

Headline: Decisions under disruption are unclear, risky, and hard to justify

Points:

- Supply chain disruptions are frequent: weather, geopolitics, port issues.
- Decision-making is often fragmented, manual, reactive.
- High uncertainty: cost vs delay vs legal risk.
- Decisions must often be justified after the fact.

## Slide 3 - Why Now

Headline: Increasing volatility + data complexity

Points:

- More frequent disruptions: global instability, climate, congestion.
- More data, but less clarity.
- Organizations lack structured decision support.
- Growing need for explainable, auditable decisions.

## Slide 4 - Our Approach

Headline: Structured decision intelligence layer

Points:

- Ingest disruption signals: events, weather, etc.
- Structure the decision context.
- Generate scenario options: wait, reroute, alternative port.
- Provide time impact, cost estimation, legal considerations.

## Slide 5 - Demo (Visual)

Headline: Example: Disruption scenario (Hamburg / Red Sea)

Show:

- Map with vessels and disruption.
- Scenario outputs: delay, cost, decision options.

Key message: Making the decision process visible and structured.

## Slide 6 - Target Niche

Headline: Initial focus: Maritime / Supply Chain Disruptions

Points:

- Hamburg as a strategic logistics hub.
- Clear decision types: reroute, wait, adjust delivery.
- Observable events and accessible signals.
- Expandable to warehousing and manufacturing.

Note: This deck outline uses a maritime-first framing. Keep it consistent with the current MVP stance (manufacturing decision support) or explicitly label it as a broader narrative variant.

## Slide 7 - Validation & Learning Approach

Headline: Grounded in real-world insights

Points:

- Direct conversations with supply chain managers and logistics planners.
- Focus on real disruption cases, decision workflows, and pain points.
- Use the demo to facilitate discussions and validate assumptions.

## Slide 8 - Go-to-Market (Early)

Headline: Network-driven discovery

Points:

- LinkedIn outreach and thought sharing.
- Industry meetups in Hamburg.
- Expert calls and mentoring.
- Leverage the local ecosystem.

## Slide 9 - Use of Funds (EUR 2,000)

Headline: Focused on validation and credibility

Points:

- Industry access and networking.
- Demo and prototype development.
- Pitch and communication materials.
- AI tools for research and structuring.
- Mentorship and expert feedback.

Key message: Spend to learn, not to overbuild.

## Slide 10 - Vision

Headline: The operating system for high-stakes decisions

Points:

- Start with supply chain disruptions.
- Expand to operations, logistics, and risk management.
- Build structured, explainable, auditable decision systems.

## Presenter Notes (Guidance)

- Emphasize problem + insight over tech.
- Keep the demo simple but legible.
- Say: we are validating the decision layer, not building full AI yet.
