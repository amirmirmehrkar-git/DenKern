# Key Risks

## Primary Risk

Can customers quantify cost-of-delay in a defensible way within 1-2 weeks without heavy consulting or ERP integration?

If not, the value proposition weakens.

## Why This Matters

DenkKern's financial impact engine depends on credible cost-of-delay assumptions. If customers cannot provide or accept these numbers quickly, the MVP may require too much consulting to sell or onboard.

The current priority is not prediction accuracy by itself. The priority is proving that prediction can be connected to a trusted, financially meaningful decision.

## Related Risks

- Operators may not have clear action paths during disruptions.
- Freight alternatives may not be available quickly enough.
- Replacement sourcing may be too manual or company-specific.
- Buyers may like the concept but resist paying before proof.
- Trust may fail if predictions are not well calibrated or explained.
- Security and data access concerns may slow onboarding.
- The demo may drift into a generic platform if the team adds features before validating the first workflow.
- GTM may become too broad if DenkKern is described as a generic AI supply chain optimization platform.
- Talking too early to logistics giants may pull the product toward visibility and shipping optimization instead of manufacturer decision support.
- Premature optimization risk: selecting advanced optimization algorithms before objectives, constraints, and real decision workflows are validated.

## Risk Reduction Path

Prioritize interviews and clickable workflow tests that reveal:

- Current disruption response behavior.
- Available cost-of-delay data.
- Decision authority.
- Budget owner.
- Pilot buying criteria.
- Minimum trust requirements.

## Current Workflow Risk

If the team does not understand the customer's current workflow, DenkKern may solve the wrong operational bottleneck. The product must learn how teams currently use phone calls, Excel, SAP screenshots, email chains, escalation meetings, and intuition during disruptions.

## Bad ICP Risk

Avoid spending early validation cycles on customers with weak MVP fit:

- Pure logistics providers.
- Low-margin manufacturers with low downtime cost.
- Companies with huge inventory buffers.
- SMEs with low operational complexity.
- Enterprises that require deep ERP integration from day one.
