# Budget Allocation Plan (EUR 2,000)

## Objective

At this stage, the primary goal is to reduce the highest risk:

Validating real-world use cases and understanding decision-making workflows under disruption.

Focus:

- Industry access.
- Structured learning.
- Credible prototyping.
- Clear problem definition.

Not heavy technical development.

## 1) Industry Access, Networking & Validation - EUR 900

Purpose: gain direct access to stakeholders, build relationships, and validate real-world problems.

Allocation:

- Coffee meetings / travel: EUR 200-300.
- Industry events / meetups (Hamburg / Germany): EUR 150-250.
- 1-2 targeted expert calls: EUR 200-400.
- LinkedIn Premium / outreach tools (optional): EUR 50-100.

Activities:

- Active LinkedIn outreach and content sharing.
- Participation in local industry meetups.
- Building visibility within supply chain and logistics communities.

Expected outcome:

- 5-10 real disruption cases.
- Network of industry contacts.
- Early credibility in the ecosystem.
- Insight into decision makers (individual vs group), workflows, and pain points.

## 2) Demo, Prototype & Problem Visualization - EUR 350

Purpose: create a simple but credible visual demo that helps communicate the problem and solution.

Allocation:

- Domain and hosting.
- UI tools / templates.
- Map integration (Leaflet or other lightweight tools).

Expected outcome:

- Interactive demo showing disruption scenarios, affected vessels / assets, and decision options (time, cost, legal).

## 3) Pitch Deck, Communication & Visibility - EUR 300

Purpose: present a clear, professional, insight-driven narrative.

Allocation:

- Pitch deck template.
- Design polish (optional freelancer).

Activities:

- Sharing insights on LinkedIn.
- Using demo visuals in conversations.
- Preparing materials for mentors and incubator reviews.

Expected outcome:

- High-quality pitch deck.
- Clear articulation of problem, niche, and early validation insights.

## 4) AI Tools for Research, Analysis & Problem Definition - EUR 200

Purpose: accelerate research, structure insights, and define the problem clearly.

Tools:

- AI tools such as ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude.
- Note-taking and structuring tools such as Notion.

Usage:

- Summarizing interviews.
- Structuring decision workflows.
- Generating scenario logic.
- Supporting report writing and insight extraction.

Expected outcome:

- Structured interview reports.
- Clearly defined problem statements.
- Better internal alignment.

## 5) Mentorship & Expert Guidance - EUR 150

Purpose: gain high-quality feedback and direction from experienced professionals.

Allocation:

- Targeted mentoring sessions.
- Expert feedback on product direction and positioning.

Expected outcome:

- Faster decision-making.
- Avoiding common startup mistakes.
- Better strategic clarity.

## 6) Contingency / Buffer - EUR 100

Purpose: remain flexible for unexpected opportunities:

- High-value event.
- Additional expert call.
- Important introduction.

## What We Intentionally Avoid

We are not investing in:

- Hardware (laptops, etc.).
- Heavy infrastructure.
- Expensive proprietary datasets.
- Early hiring.

Rationale: the priority is validating the problem and building the right foundation before scaling.

## Expected Deliverables

By the end of this phase:

- Credible visual demo.
- 5-10 validated real-world use cases.
- Active presence in the industry network (LinkedIn plus events).
- Structured problem definition.
- Clear niche selection.
- Strong pitch deck backed by real insights.

## Strategic Principle

Use this budget to learn faster, connect deeper, and build credibility early, not to overbuild prematurely.
