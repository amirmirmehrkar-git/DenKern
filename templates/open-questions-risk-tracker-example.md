## Question / Risk
> Can the customer calibrate a defensible cost-of-delay model within 2 weeks without consulting support?

---

## Context
> The North Star metric depends on knowing the avoided cost of disruption. If we cannot establish a baseline quickly, we cannot measure value accurately.

---

## Hypothesis
> Mid-sized manufacturers can provide sufficient cost-of-delay data in under 2 weeks if we provide structured guidance.

---

## Validation Method
> Conduct 3–5 customer interviews and run a mini pilot with manual intake of cost data.

---

## Who to Ask / Responsible
- Operations Manager, Hamburg shipbuilding company
- Production Manager
- Finance lead for downtime costs

---

## Evidence / Findings
- Initial interviews indicate production managers know rough downtime costs but cannot give exact numbers.
- Finance leads need structured forms to calculate defensible baseline.

---

## Risk / Impact if Unanswered
- **Financial:** High — cannot measure avoided-cost ROI
- **Product:** High — recommendation engine loses credibility
- **Timeline:** High — pilot timeline slips if baseline not collected

---

## Next Steps / Action Items
- Create a structured cost-intake form
- Schedule interviews with 3–5 key contacts
- Record and analyze data for baseline calibration
